Mindpalace

My co-founder and I developed Mindpalace, a digital second brain that lets users save insights from any media format (podcasts, articles, etc.). As CTO, I led the technical side of the product. I conducted user interviews, surveys, and usage analyses and led the full-stack development of the mobile and desktop apps. I won pitching awards, raised funds, and we were accepted to multiple accelerators.

Project Partners: Luke Haliburton, Yannick Wojcicki

Published: App Store & Play Store

Tech Stack: TypeScript, React Native, React, Node.js, AWS

VIDEO Pitch Insta

Summary: These days, people consume more content than ever before. This is more than just entertainment, people are interested in learning and personal growth. Between reading articles on the train and listening to podcasts while we walk. We are taking in information at an astonishing rate in an effort to learn new things. But you likely do not remember the interesting fact you heard on that podcast last week, nor the statistic in that article that you wanted to tell your coworker about. It turns out, more than two thirds of the things we learn are gone the very next day. It takes a lot of effort to review things and lock them into memory. And the process is not well integrated into our daily routines. In a a perfect world, every interesting thing that you encounter never gets lost. Instead, you turn it into knowledge that you remember, so you can use it when you need it. Thatâ€™s where Mindpalace comes in. Mindpalace is a digital tool that helps users get more knowledge from their content. Anywhere on the web, users can save content into their Mindpalace Then, instead of that information being lost in the heap, we actively engage users so they learn and remember their content Based on the content users save, we make inspiring personalized recommendations of new content for them to explore. Altogether Mindpalace is a digital Knowledge Platform.
